Serve

"It's all in the toss"

Preperation

As Roger Federer said "it's all in the toss, the rest is just mechanics". This is absolutely true. Once you get the service motion down you rarely ever have to think about it again and the only variable it the toss.Start by standing sideways to the court. Toss the ball up in front of you and not into the court, just high enough so that it's out of your reach. Your racket hand should move behind your shoulder in preparation to strike. Move your feet together and bend your knees.

Contact

As the ball drops down into the strike zone reach up to hit it. Pushing up with your knees will give you more access to power. Try and visualise where you want the ball to land in the moments before you hit it. Be careful not to wait too long and hit the ball low as it becomes very hard to control at that point.

Follow Though

Your weight should be moving into the court at this point. Stop it with your non-dominate foot and ready yourself for the return. Game on!
