Backhand

Most players weakest stroke

Preperation

This tutorial will cover the steps of a two handed backhand.Early preparation is key with the two hander. Once you feel you have positioned yourself correctly rotate your upper body away from the ball and "hand" the racket into your non-dominate hand. You should be looking at the ball over your shoulder and bending your knees slightly.

Contact

Let the racket head drop down to around knee height and start moving the racket forward to meet the ball. It should be at this point following a similar path as the forehand (both upward and forward). Try not to pull too much on the racket with your dominate hand, but use it to guide the racket and push with the non-dominate.

Follow Though

Keep the racket head moving upwards and over your shoulder after contact to help generate topspin. Your weight should almost entirely be on your front foot at this point, so bring your back foot around to stabilize and get ready for the next shot!
